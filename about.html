Hello There

Statement

Function and beauty have always been at the forefront of my creative process. Before I even knew that these were fundamentals of design, I was trying to achieve them. I wanted my art to have a purpose. Being a pretty picture is a valid enough purpose but I’m the type who feels bad about buying things that’ll only sit somewhere. Design allows something to do more than just sit there. That something can communicate and connect.

I use vibrant colors, clean lines, and whimsical imagery– all to communicate who I am and how I am connected with the world. I try to bring out the colors I feel when I think of the imagery I illustrate as well as including elements (although not entirely realistic) that remind me of what the image feels like. I often think of artists like Marian Bantjes, David Carson, and Mike Perry. Boiled down, it's trying to balance what it is and what it feels like.

Bio

Lily Forman is a dynamic graphic designer from the Bay Area with a flair for creating vibrant, colorful, and playful designs. She specializes in branding, promotional designs, web design, illustration, and product design. Lily is proficient in tools like Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, InDesign, and Figma, as well as having experience in HTML and CSS coding. She brings a fresh and energetic perspective to every project she works on.

Lily will graduate from the College of San Mateo in May 2025 with an AA in Graphic Design. In addition to her design skills, she has a diverse background in crafts such as painting, sewing, and jewelry making, which enriches her creative process. Guided by her endeavor toward functionality and beauty Lily brings passion and commitment to all the projects she works on.
